About

I'm an AI and data engineering leader working in healthcare — the part of the industry where getting it wrong isn't a bad quarter, it's a person.

The short version

Fourteen years across Fortune 100 healthcare, financial services, and technology. I lead AI and data engineering programs at Medtronic's AI Center of Excellence, spanning multiple operating units under HIPAA and FDA regulation. I'm a named inventor on a granted US patent for AI-driven cardiac arrhythmia detection, with further healthcare analytics patents pending.

I started in analytics — JPMorgan Chase, IBM, then Citi, where I led consumer banking analytics across three lines of business. The through-line has always been the same: take something statistically interesting and make it operationally real.

What I actually care about

The interesting problem in enterprise AI stopped being the model years ago. It's everything around it — the annotation infrastructure, the governance, the question of who signs off and on what evidence. In a regulated environment, a model nobody can audit is a model nobody can ship, so the controls aren't friction around the work. They are the work.

I'm also unreasonably interested in building the thing rather than specifying it. The agent fleet on this site exists because I'd rather demonstrate what agentic AI does to a workflow than write another slide about it. That habit has been more professionally useful than anything else I do.

Outside the day job

I founded AI for Her ATX, a 501(c)(3) in Austin, and chair its board. Its whole premise is that women in AI are not short of advice — they're short of access, and sponsorship is the thing that actually moves people into senior rooms.

The rest of what I build tends to come from the same instinct: MindPulse for health problems the industry under-serves, research into who healthcare AI is built by and trained on, and Mbrain to keep the whole fleet honest.
