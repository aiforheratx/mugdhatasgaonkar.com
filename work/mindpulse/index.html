Product · In development

MindPulse

An iOS companion for women's mental health, covering the conditions consumer health apps tend to skip — PMDD, perimenopause, fertility, and the way they tangle with anxiety, depression, ADHD, and autism.

Why this one

Women's health has a data problem that's really a priorities problem. The conditions that shape daily life for millions of women are the ones with the thinnest tooling, the vaguest guidance, and the least research behind them. PMDD is a good example: it's debilitating, it's diagnosable only through prospective daily ratings across cycles, and almost nothing helps you actually collect those ratings.

This is also the seam I know professionally. I spend my working life on clinical AI in a regulated environment. MindPulse is what happens when I point that at a problem I'd actually use.

How it's built

  • SwiftUI, native. A health companion that stutters is a health companion you stop opening.
  • On-device AI first. Apple's FoundationModels run locally, so the most sensitive category of data anyone has doesn't leave the phone to be useful.
  • HealthKit, plus wearables. HealthKit-first for Apple Watch and RingConn, with Oura available behind a config gate.
  • A learned cycle-prediction engine that adapts to the person rather than assuming a textbook 28 days.
  • Clinician PDF export — prospective PMDD ratings in the form a doctor can actually use in an appointment.

Privacy as architecture

The privacy posture isn't a policy page, it's a build decision. On-device inference means there's no server-side pile of women's mental-health and fertility data to breach, subpoena, or quietly monetise — because it doesn't exist. You can't leak what you never collected.

The app also carries hard medical guardrails. It's a companion for tracking and self-knowledge. It doesn't diagnose, and it's careful never to imply that it does.

Status: in development, approaching market readiness, with a full unit test suite green. Not yet released, and not a medical device. If you work in women's health and want to talk about it, I'd like to hear from you.
