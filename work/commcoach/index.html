Tool · Voice & audio

CommCoach

A communication coach in two halves: it turns the documents you're supposed to have read into audiobooks, and it listens back while you practise saying things out loud.

The problem

Two things I could never make time for. The reading pile — papers and books that stay unread because sitting down with them competes with everything else. And spoken delivery, which is the one skill that only improves by doing it badly first, ideally where nobody is watching.

What it does

  • PDF → audiobook. A FastAPI service that watches a folder in iCloud and turns what lands there into something I can listen to while walking.
  • A native iOS app with Siri integration, so starting a session is one sentence rather than a navigation exercise.
  • Recording and coaching — capture a run-through, get feedback on the delivery.
  • A local LLM via Ollama — no API key, no per-call cost, no audio of me practising leaving the machine.

Why local

Practice recordings are unflattering by design — that's the point of them. Shipping that audio to someone else's server so it can be transcribed and possibly retained was never a trade I wanted to make. Running the model locally removed the question entirely, and as a side effect made the thing free to use as often as I like.

There's a broader lesson I keep relearning: the cost model shapes the behaviour. When every run costs a fraction of a cent, you ration yourself without noticing. When it costs nothing, you actually practise.
