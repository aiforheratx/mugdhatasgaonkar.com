The mission

Women are well represented among people entering AI and badly represented among the people leading it. Mentorship gives someone guidance; sponsorship spends your own credibility on them — putting their name in the room they're not in, backing them for the stretch role. Advice is cheap and abundant. Access isn't. AI for Her ATX is built around the second one.

And because a young nonprofit can't afford a staff, I built it one: a small fleet of AI agents that runs the organisation's marketing, research, and operations so the humans can spend their time on the part that actually needs humans — opening doors.

The LinkedIn pipeline, end to end

The org's public voice is mostly LinkedIn. Here's how a post happens — and no post ever goes out without a human clicking approve.

  1. Gather signal

    SAGE runs deep research twice a week; IRIS scans the AI landscape on Mondays. Their briefs become the week's themes — women in AI leadership, sponsorship, women's health & AI.

    SAGE · IRIS

  2. Draft

    NOVA wakes daily at 7:00 CST and writes the day's LinkedIn content with Claude — copy in the org's voice, plus an image concept.

    NOVA + Claude

  3. Human approval

    Every draft lands in the dashboard's approval queue. A human reads, edits, approves — or kills it. The queue is the contract.

    Board

  4. Publish

    Approved posts go out through the LinkedIn API; Instagram publishes through Mbrain's central connector.

    Pipeline

  5. Log & learn

    ARIA files activity hourly through the working day; ATHENA watches the treasury. KPIs land back on the dashboard and shape next week's themes.

    ARIA · ATHENA

How Claude is used

  • claude_clientOne shared module is the fleet's entire reasoning layer — every agent's writing and analysis goes through it, so voice and guardrails live in exactly one place.
  • nova·draftingNOVA prompts Claude with the week's research themes and the org's voice guide to draft each LinkedIn post — research in, on-brand copy out, never auto-published.
  • sage·researchSAGE uses Claude for twice-weekly deep research runs that become the evidence base behind the content.
  • built_withThe framework itself — agents, dashboard, queue — was built and is maintained with Claude Code.

What is used
