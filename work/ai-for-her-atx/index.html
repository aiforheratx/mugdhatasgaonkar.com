AI for Her ATX

A 501(c)(3) nonprofit in Austin, Texas, working to get more women into senior AI roles — and to keep them there.

aiforheratx.org Instagram

The problem

Women are well represented among people entering AI and badly represented among the people leading it. The gap doesn't close by itself, and it doesn't close through advice alone.

Sponsorship, not mentorship

Mentorship gives someone guidance. Sponsorship spends your own credibility on them — putting their name in the room they're not in, backing them for the stretch role, making the introduction that changes the trajectory.

Advice is cheap and abundant. Access isn't. AI for Her ATX is built around the second one, because that's the part that's actually scarce.

What we're building

  • A founding cohort — a small, deliberate first group rather than a large mailing list.
  • Sponsorship pathways — connecting women in AI to people with the standing to open doors, not just to offer feedback.
  • A public presence — a live site and an active Instagram, so the work is visible to the people it's meant to reach.

My role

Founder and Board Chair. In practice that has meant everything from incorporating the 501(c)(3) and assembling the board through to writing the automation that runs the organisation's content and operations day to day — a small fleet of agents handling marketing, research, and scheduling so the nonprofit can run lean.

Early stage. The organisation is young and building its founding cohort. If sponsorship parity in AI is something you care about, I'd like to hear from you — get in touch.
