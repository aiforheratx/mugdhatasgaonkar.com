Women & AI in Healthcare R&D

A standing research pipeline on a two-sided question: who builds healthcare AI, and whose bodies is it trained on? The two problems turn out to be the same problem.

The question

Healthcare AI inherits the gaps in the research beneath it. Where women have been under-represented in trials and datasets, models learn a version of medicine that fits them less well — and the people best placed to notice are frequently not in the room where the model gets signed off.

Representation among builders and representation in data aren't two separate diversity talking points. They're one feedback loop, and it runs in both directions.

How Claude is used

  • deep_researchClaude is the analyst: each Monday run sweeps current research and industry signal on the week's topic and synthesises it into a long-form, structured report.
  • citationsThe pipeline's rule is that Claude must keep receipts — claims arrive with their sources, so the output is checkable rather than persuasive.
  • compoundingBecause the agent runs headless on a schedule, the knowledge base grows every week whether I have a free weekend or not — 30 documents and counting.

Why I keep it running

It feeds everything else. It's the evidence base behind AI for Her ATX's argument that sponsorship — not another mentorship programme — is the intervention that moves the number, and it's the research the nonprofit's LinkedIn content draws on. It's the reason MindPulse targets the conditions it does. Conviction without evidence is just a strongly-held opinion.
