Women & AI in Healthcare R&D

A standing research pipeline on a two-sided question: who builds healthcare AI, and whose bodies is it trained on? The two problems turn out to be the same problem.

The question

Healthcare AI inherits the gaps in the research beneath it. Where women have been under-represented in trials and datasets, models learn a version of medicine that fits them less well — and the people best placed to notice that are frequently not in the room where the model gets signed off.

Representation among builders and representation in data aren't two separate diversity talking points. They're one feedback loop, and it runs in both directions.

How it runs

It's an agent pipeline rather than a reading list. It runs on a schedule, pulls current research and industry signal, and produces a weekly report and a white-paper track — so the work compounds instead of restarting every time I get a free weekend.

  • Scheduled cloud agents doing the collection, so it continues without me.
  • A weekly report landing by email, plus a Monday dashboard summary.
  • A white-paper track — the durable output, aimed at people who can act on it.

Why I keep it running

It feeds everything else. It's the evidence base behind AI for Her ATX's argument that sponsorship — not another mentorship programme — is the intervention that moves the number. It's the reason MindPulse targets the conditions it does. Conviction without evidence is just a strongly-held opinion.

Status: ongoing, with the white-paper track in progress. Nothing published yet — when the first paper lands, it'll be linked here.
