What I do

Most of my work sits at the seam between AI research and the operational reality of shipping it: annotation infrastructure, model governance, and the cross-functional programs that move a model from a promising notebook to something a regulated business can actually depend on.

At Medtronic I lead AI and data engineering programs within the AI Center of Excellence, spanning multiple operating units in a HIPAA- and FDA-regulated environment. Before that, analytics leadership at Citi, IBM, and JPMorgan Chase.

Outside that, I found the questions I most want to answer are my own — so I build. A nonprofit for sponsorship parity in AI. An app for health problems the industry under-serves. A command centre for the agents that run all of it.

How I lead

Four things I keep coming back to, across fourteen years and four organisations.

01

Build it to prove it

I don't pitch AI strategy I haven't tested. The agent fleet on this site exists because arguing about what's possible is slower than showing it.

02

Governance is the product

In healthcare, a model nobody can audit is a model nobody can ship. The controls aren't overhead on the work — they are the work.

03

Sponsorship over advice

Advice is abundant and cheap. Access isn't. I'd rather spend my credibility on someone than spend an hour telling them what I'd do.

04

Ship, then sharpen

Most of what I've built started rough and got better in contact with reality. Perfect plans lose to working systems.
