Day job · Medtronic

Leading AI annotation & segmentation for healthcare data

Before a healthcare model can learn anything, someone has to teach the data to speak — outlining the anatomy, flagging the anomaly, drawing the boundary between signal and noise, at scale, to a standard a regulator can audit.

That's what I lead at Medtronic's AI Center of Excellence: the annotation and segmentation programs that turn raw clinical data into model-ready ground truth, an AI-first annotation framework, and the human-in-the-loop review and governance that make it hold up in a HIPAA/FDA world.

The pipeline pattern is the one animating on the right: detect, segment, label, review. The craft is making it honest at scale.

How I lead

Four things I keep coming back to, across fourteen years and four organisations.

01

Build it to prove it

I don't pitch AI strategy I haven't tested. The agent fleet on this site exists because arguing about what's possible is slower than showing it.

02

Governance is the product

In healthcare, a model nobody can audit is a model nobody can ship. The controls aren't overhead on the work — they are the work.

03

Sponsorship over advice

Advice is abundant and cheap. Access isn't. I'd rather spend my credibility on someone than spend an hour telling them what I'd do.

04

Ship, then sharpen

Most of what I've built started rough and got better in contact with reality. Perfect plans lose to working systems.
